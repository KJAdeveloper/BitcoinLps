Executive Summary

This enhanced business plan outlines the creation of a Bitcoin Treasury Company structured as a Limited Partnership (LP) to provide institutional-grade Bitcoin investment services while maximizing tax efficiency and operational flexibility. The LP structure offers significant advantages over individual investment approaches, including enhanced tax benefits, professional management capabilities, and scalable operations.

✓ Fact-Checked: LP Structure Legitimacy

Verification: Limited Partnerships are governed by IRC Sections 701-777 and state partnership laws. LPs are legitimate business structures commonly used for investment funds, real estate, and alternative investments. The IRS recognizes LPs as pass-through entities under Subchapter K.

Sources: IRC Section 701, Uniform Limited Partnership Act (ULPA), SEC Investment Company Act exemptions

Enhanced LP Tax Advantages

1. No Wash Sale Rules on Cryptocurrency

Unlike traditional securities, cryptocurrency transactions are not subject to the wash sale rules under IRC Section 1091, allowing for tax-loss harvesting strategies where losses can be realized and positions immediately re-established.

✓ Fact-Checked: Crypto Wash Sale Rules

Current Status: IRC Section 1091 wash sale rules apply only to "stocks or securities." The IRS has not classified cryptocurrency as securities for tax purposes (IRS Notice 2014-21).

Strategy: LPs can harvest losses on Bitcoin positions and immediately repurchase, optimizing tax efficiency.

Risk: Proposed legislation may extend wash sale rules to crypto. Monitor HR 5376 and similar bills.

2. Section 199A Qualified Business Income Deduction

LPs engaged in qualified business activities may be eligible for up to 20% deduction on qualified business income under IRC Section 199A, significantly reducing effective tax rates.

✓ Fact-Checked: Section 199A Benefits

Verification: IRC Section 199A allows 20% deduction on qualified business income for pass-through entities.

Requirements: Must constitute a trade or business under Section 162, not merely investment activity.

Limitation: Specified Service Trade or Business (SSTB) limitations may apply if providing investment advisory services.

Strategy: Structure operations to qualify as active business rather than passive investment.

3. Carried Interest Treatment

General Partners can receive performance-based compensation (carried interest) that qualifies for capital gains treatment after a 3-year holding period, rather than ordinary income tax rates.

✓ Fact-Checked: Carried Interest Rules

Legal Basis: IRC Section 1061 governs carried interest treatment.

Requirement: 3-year holding period for capital gains treatment (increased from 1 year by Tax Cuts and Jobs Act).

Benefit: Performance fees taxed at capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20%) vs. ordinary income rates (up to 37%).

Compliance: Must meet partnership interest requirements and holding period tests.

4. Estate Tax Benefits

LP interests can be valued at significant discounts for estate and gift tax purposes due to lack of control and marketability restrictions, enabling efficient wealth transfer strategies.

✓ Fact-Checked: LP Valuation Discounts

Legal Precedent: Courts consistently recognize valuation discounts for LP interests (Estate of Jones v. Commissioner, Estate of Holman v. Commissioner).

Typical Discounts: 20-40% discounts common for lack of control and marketability.

Requirements: Must have legitimate business purpose and respect entity formalities.

Recent Changes: IRC Section 2704 proposed regulations (currently withdrawn) attempted to limit discounts.

5. State Tax Planning Opportunities

LPs can be domiciled in states with favorable tax treatment, potentially eliminating state income taxes on partnership income.

✓ Fact-Checked: State Tax Benefits

No-Tax States: Delaware, Nevada, Wyoming, Texas, Florida offer favorable LP treatment.

Sourcing Rules: Partnership income generally sourced to state of partnership, not partner residence.

Considerations: Must have substantial business activities in chosen state to avoid "doing business" in other states.

Compliance: File appropriate state returns and maintain registered office.
